Introduction

This quarter, we were tasked with coming up with a product that improved the well-being and happiness of middle aged women. As a psychology major, I wanted to focus on the aspect of mental health, and ways to maintain and foster positive mental health. I believe that monitoring one's emotions and thoughts is important to improving well-being, and after thinking of things that can help improve mental health in the technological sphere, I stumbled upon journaling and mood-tracking apps. Journaling is a proven way to bolster happiness, as it can be used for an outlet to manage stress and anxiety, and a way to see patterns in one's mental health. In addition, digitized journaling and mood tracking in particular provides some additional benefits to its physical counterpart, such as the potential for efficiency and ease, lack of physical materials, and more.


Design statement

Upon my initial research of mood tracking and journaling apps, I found a few key problems. The first was that I often felt overwhelmed with the multitude of extraneous features provided in an app that was supposed to be about journaling, such as meditative mantras or yoga. Not only that, but many apps seemed dated, buggy, and not exactly user-friendly in terms or mindful of my time due to all the extra features. Additionally, I felt as though I couldn't really customize or personalize my entries the way I wanted, such as by adding supplemental information like pictures that would make my thoughts and memories more fulfilling to write about.


These are key problems as older users may abandon apps that could actually help them if they aren't straightforward. Furthermore, adults are busy, and sometimes don't have the minutes to spare on extra features they didn't ask for in the first place. Finally, with the addition of personalization, users can get a holistic and more fulfilling record of their thoughts, memories, and everyday lives as they journal. As a result, this project aimed to resolve those issues by creating a user-friendly app that was streamlined. I wanted to create an project that skipped all the extra features to get users through the core use, journaling and mood tracking, as quickly or slowly as they needed, with all the special touches they wanted to feel fulfilled in their mental health outlet.
